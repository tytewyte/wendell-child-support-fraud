I. TITLE IV-D FEDERAL INCENTIVE PROGRAM
================================================================================

WHAT IS TITLE IV-D?
-------------------
Title IV-D of the Social Security Act (42 U.S.C. § 651 et seq.) created the 
Child Support Enforcement Program in 1975. It is a federal-state partnership 
that provides financial incentives to states for child support collections.

FEDERAL FUNDING STRUCTURE:
--------------------------
1. MATCHING FUNDS:
   - Federal government reimburses states $2 for every $3 spent on child support
   - This is "open-ended" - NO CEILING on federal reimbursement
   - The more states spend, the more federal money they receive
   - Louisiana DCFS gets 66% of costs reimbursed by federal government

2. INCENTIVE PAYMENTS:
   - States receive ADDITIONAL incentive payments based on performance
   - Performance measured by: collections, paternity establishment, order establishment
   - The MORE they collect, the MORE federal money they get
   - These are BONUS payments on top of the 66% reimbursement

3. COST-EFFECTIVENESS BONUSES:
   - States rewarded for "cost-effectiveness"
   - Defined as: collections divided by costs
   - Creates incentive to MAXIMIZE collections and MINIMIZE costs
   - Fabricating arrears increases collections without increasing costs

THE PERVERSE INCENTIVE:
-----------------------
States have FINANCIAL MOTIVATION to:
- CREATE or INFLATE child support arrears (real or fake)
- MAXIMIZE collections (even if fraudulent)
- FILE judgments and liens to secure "debt"
- RESIST correcting errors (would reduce federal funding)

EXAMPLE: Michigan's Numbers (from research):
- Total collections FY 2018: $1,380,738,611
- For every $1 spent, Michigan collected $1-$11
- This generates MASSIVE federal reimbursement
- Estimated TRILLIONS in federal payments to states over decades

WHAT IS A JUDGMENT LIEN?
-------------------------
From Investopedia research:

"A judgment lien is a legal claim placed on a debtor's property following a 
court ruling that enforces repayment to a creditor."

"A judgment lien is considered a NONCONSENSUAL LIEN because it is attached to 
a piece of property WITHOUT THE OWNER'S CONSENT OR AGREEMENT."

KEY CHARACTERISTICS:
1. INVOLUNTARY - No signature required
2. NO CONSENT - Debtor doesn't agree
3. IMPOSED BY LAW - Court orders it
4. ATTACHES TO PROPERTY - Like a mortgage
5. MUST BE PAID to sell or refinance property
