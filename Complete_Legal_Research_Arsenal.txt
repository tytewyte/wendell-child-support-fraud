7. RES JUDICATA (CLAIM PRECLUSION)
───────────────────────────────────

DEFINITION:
A final judgment on the merits bars further claims by parties 
or their privies based on the same cause of action.

ELEMENTS:
1. Final judgment on the merits
2. Same parties (or their privies)
3. Same cause of action
4. Competent court

APPLICATION TO YOUR CASE:
✓ Final judgment dismissing $10,150 in arrears (2006-2010)
✓ Same parties (you and Charlotte)
✓ Same cause of action (child support arrears)
✓ Competent court (Louisiana court)

RESULT:
The dismissed $10,150 CANNOT be re-claimed. It is res judicata.
Charlotte's attempt to re-claim it after her divorce violates 
settled law.

CITATION IN YOUR MOTION:
"Under the doctrine of res judicata, a final judgment on the 
merits bars further claims based on the same cause of action. 
Here, $10,150 in arrears was dismissed with the consent of the 
obligee. This dismissal was a final judgment on the merits. The 
obligee's subsequent attempt to re-claim these dismissed arrears 
after her divorce violates res judicata and constitutes fraud 
upon the court."
